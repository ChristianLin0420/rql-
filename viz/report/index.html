Offline goal-conditioned RL · 50-task benchmark report

The policy is one forward pass,
dragged toward the best nearby action.

A drifting one-step generator — the DQL v11.x line, currently v11.3 — evaluated head-to-head against RQL across all 50 OGBench single-task environments: 10 families, 3 seeds per variant (v11.2 trained to 2M steps for RQL parity; v11.3 to 1M by design, after the 2M run showed post-peak decay). This page is the living report: method, theory, live results, and the diagnostics that say why the numbers are what they are.

Thesis

Diffusion-class expressiveness does not require diffusion-class inference: amortize the iteration into training by drifting the generator toward advantage-tilted actions borrowed from neighboring states — and stitching emerges from the borrowing.

      01

      An offline policy has two jobs. Most methods buy the second with expensive inference.

      In offline goal-conditioned RL, a policy must stay on the data manifold — out-of-distribution actions meet a critic that has never seen them and will happily overestimate them — while still improving on the behavior that generated the data. The strongest recent systems resolve this tension with expressive generative policies: diffusion or flow models that can represent the multimodal action distributions real datasets contain.

      RQL — the baseline throughout this report — is one such system: a flow-based policy trained for 2M gradient steps, evaluated across all 50 OGBench single-task environments (10 families × 5 goals). Its published appendix numbers are the bar here. But expressiveness bought with an inference-time integration has costs: reconstruction error accumulates across flow steps, and the value bootstrap rides on top of it.

      The drift line asks a narrower question: can a policy that is a single forward pass keep the expressiveness and drop the iteration? The generator a = πθ(s,ε) is an implicit distribution — as multimodal as a diffusion policy, with no density, no ODE, no sampling chain. All the iteration happens at training time, where it is amortized: each SGD step moves the pushforward distribution one drift-step toward the target policy.

      The bet

      Attraction candidates come from neighboring states' actions — multi-positive, state-local, advantage-tilted. At a junction where two dataset trajectories cross, the policy can adopt whichever episode's action has higher advantage. That recombination is trajectory stitching, the core competency offline GCRL is scored on.

      The counterpoint

      Concurrent work (DriftQL, arXiv:2606.00350) builds a drift actor on the opposite premise — "offline RL is single-positive by construction": each state pulls only toward its own dataset action. The multi-positive mechanism is this project's central differentiating claim, and the diagnostics in chapter 05 are designed to test it.

      Why 50 tasks

      Single-environment wins don't survive contact with a benchmark. The 50-task OGBench suite spans locomotion (antmaze, humanoidmaze) where stitching dominates, and sparse manipulation (scene, puzzle, cube) where reward density collapses to fractions of a percent — cube-double's success density is 0.058%. A method claim has to hold across both.

      02

      A critic that can rank actions, and an actor whose drift never asks it for a gradient.

      The agent is two coupled components. The critic must be action-sharp — able to rank actions at a fixed state — and optimistic enough to carry an improvement signal. The actor must extract that signal without querying ∇aQ at actions the critic has never seen. Actions are chunks: a ∈ ℝhd flattens h consecutive atomic actions (h=1 locomotion, h=5 manipulation).

      The target the actor drifts toward

      1maxq  Ea~q[ Q(s,a) ]  −  α · KL( q(·|s) ‖ μ(·|s) )
      2π*(a|s) = (1/Z(s)) · μ(a|s) · exp( A(s,a) / α ),   A = Q − V

      In plain language: the ideal policy is the behavior distribution μ exponentially re-weighted by advantage — improve where the critic says to, but never leave what the data supports. Everything below is machinery for reaching (2) with a one-step generator.

      Critic — IQL backup, expectile value, in-support contrast

      V1LV = Ei[ |κ − 𝟙{Δi<0}| · Δi² ],   Δi = meane Q̄e(si,ai) − V(si)
      V2yi = Σj<h γj ri,j + γh V(si′)
      V3LQ = Ei,e[ (Qe(si,ai) − yi)² · validi ]
      V4LC = Ei,n[ relu( meane Qe(si,an−) − meane Qe(si,ai) + m ) ]

      In plain language: (V2)–(V3) is IQL with an h-step backup — no policy in the TD target, so nothing off-support enters the bootstrap. (V1)'s expectile κ is the improvement dial: at κ=0.5, V is the behavior mean; as κ→1, V approaches the in-support max and the backup compounds improvement recursively. (V4) is the anti-flatness lever — data actions must out-rank random and generator negatives by margin m, which keeps Q from collapsing to a function of state alone (the failure that killed v10).

      Actor — attraction, repulsion, damped ascent

      A1Aj = QLCBtarget(si,aj) − V(si);   Âj = Aj / E|A|
      A2wj = softmaxj( Âj / τadv − ‖si − sj‖² / b )
      A3mp(g) = Σj k(g,aj) wj aj / Σj k(g,aj) wj
      A4mq(g) = Σk′≠k k(g,gk′) gk′ / Σk′≠k k(g,gk′)
      A5Vdrift(g) = mp(g) − mq(g)
      A6Ldrift = Ei,k ‖ gk − sg( gk + η · Vdrift(gk) ) ‖²
      A7Lasc = −Ei,k[ QLCBtarget(si,gk) ]
      A8Lactor = Ldrift + cq · Lasc

      In plain language: for each batch state, take the M=32 nearest neighbor states and their data actions as candidates (A1–A2 weight them by normalized advantage, discounted by state distance). The drift field (A5) is a difference of two kernel-weighted means: attraction toward the tilted candidate cloud, repulsion from the generator's own samples — approximately τ²∇log(p̂w/q̂)(g), a score estimate that never touches ∇aQ. (A6) regresses the generator onto its own drifted output; the stop-gradient means no backprop through sampling. (A7) is the one exception — an explicit Q-ascent that does query the critic at generator actions — kept on a leash at cq=0.25 because at 1.0 it oscillated (chapter 03).

      Deployment Evaluation draws K=32 samples from the slow EMA generator θ̄ and deploys the medoid — the sample minimizing total squared distance to the others. Chapter 07 shows why this choice is now a suspect.

      As implemented

      agents/dql_v11_2.py · one update step (condensed) 
      # ---- critic: IQL Q + expectile V + in-support contrast ----
q_pred = Q_ensemble(s0, a_data)                  # a_data = h-step action chunk
adv    = mean(target_Q(s0, a_data)) - mean(V(s0))
L_v    = mean( where(adv>=0, expectile, 1-expectile) * adv^2 )
y      = n_step_rewards + discount^h * V(s_next) * masks
L_q    = mean( (q_pred - y)^2 * valids )
gen    = actor(s0, eps_g)  # G=16 noise draws; gsg = stop_grad(gen)
a_neg  = concat(uniform_random(n_rand=8), gsg)
L_c    = mean( relu( Q(s0, a_neg) - Q(s0, a_data) + margin ) )
critic_loss = L_q + v_coef*L_v + cql_coef*L_c

# ---- one-step drift actor ----
nn      = M=32 nearest-neighbor states in batch
a_cand  = a_data[nn];  q_im = stop_grad( LCB_targetQ(s0, a_cand) )
adv_n   = (q_im - V(s0)) / mean|adv|
w       = softmax( adv_n / adv_temp - sq_sel / (state_bw * mean(sq)) )
kern_p  = exp(-|gsg - a_cand|^2 / 2*tau2) * w    # -> mean_p
kern_q  = exp(-|gsg - gsg'|^2  / 2*tau2) * (1-I) # -> mean_q (leave-one-out)
V_drift = mean_p - mean_q
goal    = stop_grad( gen + drift_step * V_drift )
actor_loss = drift_coef * mean|gen - goal|^2 - q_coef * mean(LCB_targetQ(s0, gen))

# ---- targets / deployment ----
target_q     <- 0.005*q + 0.995*target_q
target_actor <- 0.005*actor + 0.995*target_actor  # eval uses this EMA
deploy: 32 samples from target_actor -> medoid (argmin total pairwise squared dist)

      03

      Three failures, three fixes: how v11.2 was derived, not tuned.

      Each version transition was forced by a measured failure and verified by a measured recovery. The line matters because it turns the final recipe into a set of falsifiable claims — each knob has a diagnostic that says why it holds its value.

      v10

      Action-flat critic

      The geometric-interpolation value trained toward an action-independent target — actively regularizing Q to ignore actions. Probe: rank_acc peaked 0.63 then decayed to 0.57; action/state spread ratio fell to 0.022 (V varies ~45× more across states than actions). Zero success on the cube families — 0.0 through 550–600k.

      probe ratio 0.086 → 0.022 · killed
      v11

      Critic fixed; actor exposed

      IQL transition target + margin contrast + noise-free V head. rank_acc → 1.0 and holds. But cube-double stayed 0.0 through 500k, and antmaze-large swung 0.50↔0.00 between consecutive evals — the failure moved from critic to actor.

      rank_acc 0.57 → 1.0 · still unstable
      v11.1

      Stability solved, strength capped

      Advantage-weighted drift, Q-ascent damped to cq=0.25, eval EMA (RQL's own stability trick). Eval swing fell 0.162 → 0.082 — fully stable — but performance plateaued at ~0.38 on antmaze-large versus RQL's 0.84: stable-but-weak.

      swing 0.162 → 0.082 · capped at 0.38
      v11.2

      Strength levers

      Sharper tilt (τadv 0.5→0.25), stronger pull (η 1.0→1.5), and the expectile correction: κ=0.7 instead of the 0.5 copied from RQL's flow-value regime — restoring a recursive improvement signal. Pilot: antmaze-large 0.38 → 0.73 at 100k steps.

      overall 15.8 → 18.6 @1M · decays at 2M
      v11.3 — this run

      Extraction fixes, split verdict

      Dataset-wide attraction pool + argmax-Q deployment (both checkpoint-validated). Manipulation transformed — scene 11.6→41.6, puzzle-3x3 22→48.4 — but the pool collapsed the locality term everywhere, and forced borrowing broke cyclic locomotion (humanoidmaze 61→0). The locality bandwidth must adapt per family.

      scene ×3.6 · humanoid −61 · overall 14.5
      ¶ The v11.1 cap wasn't a capacity limit — it was κ=0.5 removing recursive improvement propagation. An IQL-style critic needs an optimistic expectile to compound improvement through the bootstrap.

      The 0.73 anchor is a single-seed pilot at 100k steps, not the pre-registered gate (mean over evals ≥250k ≥ 0.5). The 50-task run on this page is the real test. Pre-registered falsification: if all three levers land at v11.1 level, the one-step drift caps out and improvement must come from a multi-step generator — the RQL-style capacity this line set out to avoid.

      05

      Two scatters that say why: critic health, and where actions are borrowed.

      A success table can't distinguish "the critic can't see" from "the actor can't extract." Two internal diagnostics, logged on every task, separate them.

      rank_acc is the fraction of uniform-random actions the data action out-ranks under QLCB at the same state — a direct probe of action-sharpness. w_self is the attraction weight a state's own action retains among its kNN candidates — when it is low, the drift target is dominated by neighboring states' actions. Low w_self is the multi-positive mechanism caught in the act.

      Critic health × action borrowing — all 50 tasks, seed 0

      Left: bottom-right points — critic near-perfect, success zero — are tasks where extraction, not evaluation, is the bottleneck. Right: if the stitching story holds, success on locomotion should coexist with low self-weight (heavy borrowing).

      ¶ rank_acc → 1 while sparse-manipulation success stays 0: critic quality is necessary but not sufficient. The remaining failures live in the actor and its deployment path.

      07

      Six propositions, honestly scored — including the two the review broke.

      The idealized picture: the drift dynamics is a kernelized Wasserstein gradient flow on the RL free energy Fs(q) = −Eq[Q] + α·KL(q‖μ), whose unique minimizer is the tilted target (2), with the equilibrium identifiable: Vp,q = 0 everywhere iff q = p = π*. Six propositions connect that picture to the implemented estimator. An external technical review (validated, 2026-07-14) corrected or retracted four of them — the corrections are kept, not hidden, because two of them generated the current best hypotheses for the remaining failures.

      P1

      Expectile κ is an improvement operator

      Claim to prove: Tκ contracts for all κ∈(0,1) and its fixed point interpolates Qμ → Q*supp as κ→1. Correction kept: κ=0.5 does not eliminate improvement — one step of exponential tilting is still valid policy improvement. What it removes is recursive propagation through the critic fixed point. The 0.38 plateau is an observation/hypothesis, not a theorem.

      corrected · open
      P2

      Softmax blur bounds the drift target's value gap

      As τadv→0 the weights go one-hot on the argmax-advantage candidate; finite τadv blends good and mediocre actions. Claim to bound: the value gap of the blend versus the argmax, in terms of τadv, advantage spread, and kernel bandwidth — plus SNIS bias at finite M=32. Review note: τadv is a bias–variance lever, not a pure sharpness win (figure F4 measures both sides).

      open — to bound
      P3

      Damped ascent has a derivable stability margin

      Sufficient condition of the form cq·‖H‖ < cd·η·k′ for non-expansive actor iteration; violating it admits limit cycles — the cq=1.0 oscillation (0.5↔0.0) observed in v11. Two empirical anchor points already exist; figure F5's grid is the test.

      open — to prove
      P4

      The implemented drift is (approximately) a WGF step

      The KDE mean-shift/score identity is exact — correction kept: but the normalized field is not automatically the exact Wasserstein gradient flow of a smoothed KL. Claim softened to: a score-difference field toward the SNIS-tilted empirical behavior, with the fixed-point gap from the ideal π* ∝ μeA/α to be characterized.

      softened
      P5

      EMA deployment is variance reduction; medoid is mode-seeking

      EMA half still open — to show: stationary points unchanged and a λ/(2−λ) variance factor, reconciled with the measured swing drop 0.162→0.082 (confounded with the simultaneous ascent damping). Medoid half refuted: Σl‖gk−gl‖² = K‖gk−ḡ‖² + const — the selector picks the sample closest to the mean, which for multimodal action distributions can deploy a between-modes compromise. Candidate contributor to the sparse-manipulation zeros; kernel-mode selector testable on saved checkpoints without retraining (figure F6).

      half refuted
      P6

      Contrast and ascent admit no degenerate equilibrium

      Retracted: false in general — a value margin does not constrain Q-gradients, so drift and ascent can cancel at a contrastive blind spot. Replaced with an empirical probe.

      retracted

      Prover priority: P1(ii)–(iii), P2's blur bound, P3's stability margin — the three that would turn the version line's empirical jumps into derived conditions. The toy-exactness panel (F8) verifies drift field, blur bias, and stability boundary against closed-form ground truth (bimodal Gaussian μ, quadratic Q) to pre-empt the estimator-versus-theory objection.

      08

      What would change the conclusions.

      1. The name is taken, and the idea is crowded. Concurrent work DriftQL (arXiv:2606.00350) owns the DQL/DriftQL name with a single-positive drift actor; DFP (arXiv:2605.07727) publishes the Wasserstein-gradient-flow framing. The project's own proposal instructs a rename. The defensible deltas are specific: multi-positive state-local advantage-tilted attraction, the optimistic-expectile h-step critic with LCB and contrast, and the damped-ascent stability + finite-sample blur analysis.
      2. The comparison is close but not fully apples-to-apples. v11.2 now matches RQL's 2M training steps, but RQL uses per-task tuning and 100M-transition datasets on two families, versus one config per family and standard v0 datasets here. The deltas remain conservative on those two families and optimistic nowhere.
      3. The plateau explanation is a hypothesis. P1(iii) as originally stated was wrong; the κ=0.5 behavior-level cap is consistent with the data but not proven. The κ ablation figure (F3) is the designed test: a behavior-shaped value map at κ=0.5 versus a distance-to-go-shaped map at κ≥0.7.
      4. Deployment may be throwing away solved tasks. The medoid selector is mean-proximal, not mode-seeking — on multimodal action distributions it can deploy a between-modes compromise. This is the live candidate explanation for tasks where rank_acc≈1 yet success is 0, and it is testable on saved checkpoints without retraining. Resolved in v11.3: deployment now takes argmax-Q_LCB over the candidates (checkpoint ablation: puzzle-3x3-t1 0.41 → 0.96–1.00).
      5. Raw-state kNN is the suspect representation for manipulation. Neighbors in raw state space may be meaningless in contact-rich scenes; critic-representation kNN is the proposed comparison.
      6. Known estimator biases are unfixed. Advantage normalization is batch-global (should be per-state with an epsilon floor); η is not the true actor step size (Adam preconditioning and the generator Jacobian intervene); weight entropy / effective sample size are not yet logged.
      7. Pre-registered kill condition. If the strength levers land at v11.1 level across the sweep, the one-step drift caps out, and improvement must come from a multi-step generator — the very capacity cost this line exists to avoid.
      09 · Bottom line

      One forward pass is either enough, or it isn't — and this run decides.

      The drift line's claim was never "beat RQL everywhere." It is that an O(1)-inference policy, drifted toward advantage-tilted actions borrowed from neighboring states, keeps enough of the expressiveness that matters — and that every remaining failure can be named: an expectile that never compounds improvement, a softmax that blurs, an ascent that oscillates, a selector that averages modes.

      The 50-task verdict renders live above as seeds complete. At its latest rebuild, the v11.1 checkpoint of this same pipeline scored 15.8 overall (completed seeds only — its sweep was still partial) against RQL's 55.5; v11.2 carries the strength levers that broke the antmaze plateau in pilot. Where the gap persists, chapters 05–08 say precisely which mechanism to indict next.
